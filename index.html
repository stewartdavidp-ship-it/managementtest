Every engine. One brain.

Any AI that supports MCP connects to the same persistent backend. Cursor for builds. Gemini for long-context analysis. ChatGPT for conversational exploration. All sharing the same decisions, rules, and context ‚Äî no re-explaining across tools.

Lifecycle Intelligence
The right tool for each task.

A surface registry tracks what each engine can do. A task router recommends the best surface for each job based on your subscriptions and actual usage. Over time, crowd-sourced data refines the recommendations ‚Äî so every user benefits from every user.

Maker / Checker
Engines verify each other.

One engine proposes. Another validates. No single AI's confident hallucination goes unchecked. Your stable of subscriptions becomes a redundancy layer ‚Äî catching errors before they reach your codebase.

ACC's architecture already supports this. The MCP backend, surface registry, and skill system were designed to be engine-agnostic from the start.
